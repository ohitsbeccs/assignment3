About Axolotls

Axolotls (Ambystoma mexicanum) are unique amphibians native to lakes around Mexico City. Known for their ability to regenerate limbs, they are often called the "Mexican walking fish" (although they're not fish!).

Natural Habitat

Axolotls are found in the lakes and canals of Xochimilco, a region near Mexico City. They thrive in freshwater environments with cool, stable temperatures and lots of underwater plants.

Axolotl Care Tips

  • Tank Size: At least 20 gallons for one axolotl.
  • Temperature: Keep between 60-64°F (16-18°C) to mimic their natural environment.
  • Water Quality: Clean, filtered water is essential; avoid strong currents.
  • Diet: Axolotls eat worms, small fish, and special axolotl pellets.

Fun Facts About Axolotls

Did you know?

  • Axolotls can regenerate entire limbs, and even parts of their heart and brain.
  • They have a rare neotenic trait, meaning they remain in their juvenile form throughout life.
  • Axolotls are critically endangered in the wild, mainly due to habitat loss.
